About Me

My name is Munaz Rahman and I am a student with an amazing passion for technology pursuing a Bachelor of Applied Science in Computer Engineering at the University of Waterloo.

My passion for technology began when I picked up a book titled Look Mom! I Built My Own Web Site from my local library. At a young age, I immediately fell in love with the ability to design and create something of my own by writing lines of code. This truly sparked my love for programming so I continued to advance my skills in other programming languages.

On top of programming I love building things using technology. I've participated in various robotic clubs, love attending hackathons and also working on projects of my own. I truly believe that technology, both through software and hardware, can be used to solve many problems faced by people today.

Projects

Smart Mirror

Using a Raspberry Pi, computer monitor and a two way mirror I created a smart mirror that displays weather, news, traffic and more while still functioning as a simple mirror. View the source code, including steps to build your own smart mirror, here.

Roomies | Android App | Node.js Backend

Roomies is an Android app with a Node.js backend service that makes it easier for roommates to live with each other by allowing them to organize chores, track expenses and share grocery lists. Find it on the Google Play Store here, view the Android source code here and backend source code here.

Meetr | DeltaHacks 2016

Meetr is an application aimed to help businesses conserve more energy by controlling the Nest smart thermostat in their office rooms based on their meeting times. By disabling the heating or air conditioning while meetings are not in effect, businesses can save more energy and money. This project was also the winner of the DeltaHacks 2016 GE Sustainability Prize. View the source code here.

Lets | Web Application | Hack the North 2015

Lets is a web application I developed for my Hack the North 2015 project. It allows students to share events publicly using a responsive website. View the source code here.

WatApp | Pebble Smart Watch App

WatApp is used by hundreds of University of Waterloo students to access the information they need on their wrists. Students can view information on campus restaurants, employer information sessions, cafeteria menus, parking availability and more. View the source code here, view it on the Pebble appstore here and download it from your smartphone here.
