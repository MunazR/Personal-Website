About Me

My name is Munaz Rahman and I am a student with an amazing passion for technology pursuing a Bachelor of Applied Science in Computer Engineering at the University of Waterloo.

My passion for technology began when I picked up a book titled Look Mom! I Built My Own Web Site from my local library when I was younger. I immediately fell in love with the ability to design and create something of my own by writing lines of code. This truly sparked my love for programming as I continued to advance my skills in other programming languages.

On top of programming I love building things using technology. I've participated in various robotic clubs and I love attending hackathons. I've worked with my team members to create innovative solutions and have also competed in different competitions. I truly believe that technology, both through software and hardware, can be used to solve many problems faced by people today.

Personal Projects

Lets | Web Application

Lets is a web application I developed for my Hack the North 2015 project. It provides a medium for students to easily share and create events with other students. It utilizes Facebook to authenticate users, Google Maps to share and choose locations and FireBase to handle all backend services without actually having a backend. View the source code here.

WatApp | Pebble Smart Watch App

WatApp provides uWaterloo students with the information they need on their wrists. Students can view information on food locations, employer information sessions, weather and more. It utilizes Pebble.js and the University of Waterloo Open Data API to perform its functionality. View the source code here, view it on the Pebble appstore here and download it from your smartphone here.

This project is my personal favorite and has led me to learn many things.
